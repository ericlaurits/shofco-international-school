We believe in the following Well-Being principles that guide our approaches to teaching a learning.

  • Academic Well-Being

    Our academic experiences place heavy emphasis on the use of technology, innovation, STEAM, and access to rigorous academics for all. We use technology with purpose and intention in the 21st century. It is a means for both creation as well as the construction of knowledge. We embrace advances in technology.

  • Social Well-Being

    Tuko pamoja - our once massive world is becoming smaller and smaller. While we strive to maintain our own culture and identity and remain uniquely Kenyan, we embrace our connections to others around the globe. We learn within and about our natural world and advocate on its behalf. We care for the earth and its people and seek both connection with and protection of natural resources.

  • Physical Well-Being

    We learn about ourselves through the exploration of physical activities, both cooperative and competitive, and learn to take care of our bodies through regular, intentional exercise. We bring mindfulness into our physical work through patience and precision.

  • Emotional Well-Being

    We express ourselves authentically through studio and performing arts, music, and writing and we reveal our true selves, advocate for change, and innovate. Our campus provides the facilities and save space to experiment, showcase, and iterate on the creative processes.
